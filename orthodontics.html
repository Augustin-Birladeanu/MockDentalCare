Adults Use Orthodontics As Well

While many people associate orthodontics with children and teens, adults can greatly benefit from these treatments too. Some adults seek orthodontics for minor spacing issues or a slight overbite, while others might need comprehensive treatment for teeth that have become misaligned due to injury or genetics. We offer several common treatments to help adults improve their smiles.

Types of Orthodontics

  • Braces: Braces are a popular orthodontic solution used to correct alignment problems like overbites, underbites, or teeth that need to be moved forward or backward. Made from metal, ceramic, or clear plastic, braces are effective for patients of all ages.
  • Invisalign: This treatment uses a series of clear, removable aligners to gradually shift your teeth into the desired position. Invisalign is an excellent choice for adults who want a discreet option, and it's also suitable for some children. Both adults and children can see significant benefits from this treatment.

Discuss Your Options Today!

Orthodontic treatment in children and teens is often a proactive measure to correct minor issues like overbites or spacing. However, adults sometimes require orthodontic treatment for more complex tooth misalignment.

The duration of orthodontic treatment can vary from a few months to several years, depending on the severity of the problem. While traditional braces are visible, Invisalign offers a discreet alternative since the aligners are nearly invisible. This means you can wear clear aligners comfortably at work or in public. During your visit to our office, we'll thoroughly discuss all your options to determine the best approach for you.

FAQs

Does getting braces hurt? What about wearing them?

When braces are first put on, you might feel a little pressure, but this discomfort usually subsides quickly. While wearing braces, maintaining excellent oral hygiene is crucial. Braces don't replace good oral hygiene; they work in conjunction with it to help you achieve a better smile. You should continue to brush twice daily, clean behind your teeth, and floss regularly.

If you notice a loose piece or feel something is wrong, it's important to see your orthodontist as soon as possible. Ignoring a loose piece could be harmful to your health if swallowed.

Will I wear a retainer when my braces come off?

Yes, if you're wondering if you'll need a retainer after your braces come off, the answer is usually yes. A retainer helps keep your teeth in their new positions, ensuring that the time and investment you put into braces result in a beautiful, lasting smile.
