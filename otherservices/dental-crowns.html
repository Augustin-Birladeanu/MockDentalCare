What is a Dental Crown?

A dental crown is a type of dental restoration used to add strength to a tooth weakened by decay or injury. A dental crown is a metal or porcelain cap implanted over a tooth to restore its shape, size, and strength.

While dental crowns are most frequently used to repair the strength of teeth, they can also be utilized to improve a tooth's aesthetics. Crowns are sometimes referred to as "caps" because they are designed to fit over or "cap" the tooth. Crowns can be fabricated from various materials, including gold, porcelain, and composite resin.

Why Get a Dental Crown?

Dental crowns are one of the most common restorative procedures at dental offices. Anyone with a tooth that is badly decayed, fractured, or affected by a disease, such as gum disease, can benefit from dental crowns.

If a tooth is badly decayed, it may have become so weak that it can no longer hold its shape. A dental crown can help restore the tooth’s size and shape, making it stronger and helping it remain functional. Dental crowns can also help protect teeth weakened by gum disease, dental restorations such as fillings, or tooth fractures.

A dental crown can be used to cap a tooth that has become weakened due to gum disease, such as periodontal disease. It can also be used to cap dental restorative materials, such as root canal fillings or dental bridge materials.

Types of Dental Crowns

Full Dental Crown

In many cases, a full dental crown is recommended. This dental crown covers the entire tooth, including the visible portion of the tooth, the root, and the gum area surrounding the tooth.

Full dental crowns are usually recommended when a tooth has been so severely decayed or damaged that restoring the tooth’s shape and structure is impossible.

Partial Dental Crown

A partial dental crown may be recommended if a tooth is badly decayed or fractured but can still be restored to its original shape. Partial dental crowns cover only the visible portion of the tooth and the root.

Partial dental crowns are usually used to restore the shape of a tooth while also covering the decayed area. They are instrumental when the damage is limited to the tooth's top.

FAQs

Is Getting a Dental Crown Right for You?

If you have a tooth that is decayed or damaged, it may be best to get a dental crown to repair the damage. A dental crown can be better than fillings because they last longer and are less likely to need repair.

You may also consider getting a dental crown if you have a tooth that has been fractured or broken and you want to protect it. If your dentist recommends getting a dental crown, they will likely provide you with details about what materials are available. You can then choose the dental crown material that best suits your needs.

Who Is a good candidate for dental crowns?

Dental crowns are an excellent option for treating damaged or decayed teeth. They can help restore the appearance of your smile, especially if your teeth are crooked or broken. A dental crown is a covering that fits over your tooth’s filling.

It protects the tooth from damage and extends its lifespan. There are many reasons why you might need a dental crown. One common reason is that decay has weakened the tooth so much that it cannot support its weight anymore.

Another reason is when the natural dentin of the tooth (the layer beneath the enamel) has eroded due to aging or trauma. Dental crowns can be custom-made to fit over any dental filling material, including composite fillings, porcelain fillings, and even gold fillings. They can also be made to match any shade of tooth color, including white and yellow teeth.
