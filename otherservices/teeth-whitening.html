If you’ve ever felt self-conscious about your yellow or stained teeth, you are not alone. A first impression is often based on a person’s smile.

Teeth play a critical role in our physical appearance and how others perceive us. If you want to boost your confidence and reveal a healthy, brilliant smile, consider getting a **teeth whitening treatment** at Chonky Dental Care.

It’s the perfect way to lighten your natural tooth color and leave you with a sparkling new grin. Read on to learn more about this popular cosmetic dental procedure.

**What is Teeth Whitening?**

The term "teeth whitening" refers to many cosmetic dental procedures used to lighten teeth. On the whole, teeth whitening involves the application of a bleaching agent to the teeth; this agent can break down the stains that build up on teeth over time, removing them from the surface of the teeth and lightening their appearance.

Some different methods can be used for teeth whitening. For instance, in-office whitening involves a dentist applying a bleaching agent to the teeth and placing a special tray over the teeth to keep the agent in place.

**How Does Teeth Whitening Work?**

The basic process of teeth whitening is that a bleaching solution is applied to the surface of the teeth. This solution breaks down the stains and discoloration of the teeth and is then rinsed off. The teeth are then given adequate time to rest before they are polished. The teeth whitening process is simple and painless, and the results are so worth it!

Teeth whitening with a dentist is a great idea if you have teeth that are so discolored that they’re affecting your self-confidence. Teeth whitening can lighten your teeth by up to eight shades and is one of the easiest and most effective ways to improve your smile.

**FAQs**

How long do teeth stay white after whitening?

Teeth whitening can last anywhere from one to two years. The exact duration of teeth whitening will depend on several factors, including your oral health and how diligent you are with teeth cleaning and maintenance. If your teeth are re-stained after one or two years, you can repeat the teeth whitening process.

To maintain the brightness of your teeth, you should brush your teeth twice a day, floss at least once a day, and visit the dentist for regular teeth cleanings.

Will Teeth Whitening Be Safe for Me?

Teeth whitening is a safe procedure, provided that it is performed by a professional in a dentist’s office. If you decide to use an at-home teeth whitening product, be aware that these products can be harsh on the gums and teeth.

Sometimes, teeth whitening at home can cause teeth to darken over time. You must visit your dentist for a teeth whitening consultation if you want whiter teeth.

Your dentist can examine your teeth, gums, and bite to determine if whitening is right for you. Your dentist can also help you choose the best whitening method for your individual needs.
