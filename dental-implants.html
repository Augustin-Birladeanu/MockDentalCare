What is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is an artificial tooth root surgically placed into your jawbone. They're used to replace a single missing tooth, an entire row of missing teeth, or to support a dental bridge. Ideal candidates are healthy enough for surgery, non-smokers, and have healthy gums.

Dental implants fuse with your jawbone, creating a strong, stable foundation for artificial teeth. They are a durable, long-term solution for lost teeth and an excellent option for individuals who may not be candidates for traditional tooth replacement methods like bridges or dentures.

Implants are also a great choice for those who have had root canal therapy, as they allow you to replace a single missing tooth without worrying about it breaking or shifting in your mouth.

Why Should I Get a Dental Implant?

If you're missing one or more teeth, a dental implant can be an excellent way to improve your oral health. An implant provides a strong foundation for a replacement tooth made of porcelain or other durable materials. They are exceptionally durable and can last for many years.

Depending on the type, dental implants can replace a single missing tooth or support a dental bridge. They are a permanent solution for lost teeth and a fantastic option for individuals who aren't suitable candidates for traditional tooth replacement methods like a bridge or denture.

Dental implants are also beneficial for individuals who've had root canal therapy, enabling the replacement of a single missing tooth without concerns about it breaking or moving.

Types of Dental Implants

  • Single-tooth implant: Used to replace one missing tooth. This type of implant can also support a dental bridge if other teeth are present.
  • Implant Bridge: This type of dental implant replaces a row of missing teeth. It consists of multiple teeth supported by one or more implants.

What are the advantages of dental implants?

Dental implants offer numerous advantages that make them a worthwhile consideration for your oral health needs:

  • Improved aesthetics
  • Natural-looking teeth
  • Improved chewing ability
  • No need for permanent dentures
  • Avoiding jawbone deterioration
  • Restoring facial aesthetics
  • Improved self-confidence and quality of life
  • No risk of gum disease associated with the implant itself
